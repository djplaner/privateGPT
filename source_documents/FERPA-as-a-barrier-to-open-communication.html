FERPA as a barrier to open communication

Tags: Uncategorized, web 2.0 course sites

A discussion on a mailing list indicates that government legislation may be a barrier to the Web 2.0 idea of making everything open.  In someway related to, or tied up with the issue of privacy etc. In the discussion some are worried that the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA)  means that online discussions for a course can't be made completely open. I'm not a lawyer but reading the page I wonder if participation in a online discussion is actually part of the student record. Either way the point is that having everything completly open is not always possible.
