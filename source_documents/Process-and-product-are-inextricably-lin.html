The process used to design something, influences the end result. The nature of the process used, to some extent, limits what can be produced. Earlier I wrote about my concerns around traditional instructional design as practiced at CQU in the late 1990s and how I thought participatory design might offer a different approach. This quote from Simon has a nice resonance with this idea. It also provides a potential frame for a publication comparing the original approach with the new approach (whatever form that takes) and then doing a comparison of the outcomes.
