Unintended consequences of technology in education

Tags: bad


   by  woodleywonderworks 
My wife is currently studying engineering. One of her fellow engineering students is also studying some mathematics on the side. He shared the following tale of unintended consequences arising from technology in the education. In a particular mathematics course the students are set homework. The lecturer will then work through the solutions of these homework problems in the next lecture. This is done by using the solutions manual (provided by the publisher I assume) and the document camera available in the lecture theatre. Small problem. Can you pick it? Apparently the lecture is recorded and made available online. Also, the lecturer has a habit of flicking through the solutions manual to find the right page whilst the manual is under the document camera. It appears that the students have discovered you can pause the online video recording and take a good hard look at what's revealed.
